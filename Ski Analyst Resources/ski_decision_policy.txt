Ski Day Decision Policy

Core principles:
- Structured resort operations data and mountain report are the primary signals.
- Weather and surface conditions matter more than same-day snowfall alone.
- If trails are open, assume there is adequate on-trail snow coverage.
- Rain, ice, strong wind, and operational disruption are the most important negative factors.
- Same-day snowfall is a quality booster, not a requirement for skiing.

Operational interpretation rules:
- Trails open > 0 means the resort has sufficient snow coverage on those trails.
- Do NOT interpret 0 cm snowfall as “no snow exists.”
- Snowfall mainly affects snow quality (softness, freshness), not basic skiability.
- If lifts are 0% open but trails are open:
  - Check mountain report text for explanation:
    - off-hours / pre-opening
    - wind holds
    - mechanical issues
    - delayed operations
    - temporary closure
  - If no clear explanation → reduce confidence, do NOT immediately label as No Go.

Factor guidelines:

Terrain availability:
- High:
  - Jan–Feb: >= 60% open
  - Dec/March: >= 50% open
- Medium:
  - Jan–Feb: 35–59%
  - Dec/March: 30–49%
- Low:
  - below those thresholds
- Note:
  - Reflects usable terrain, not snowfall.

Snow quality:
- High:
  - fresh snowfall OR
  - no rain damage + stable cold conditions
- Medium:
  - packed powder, groomed snow, older snow, mixed conditions
- Low:
  - rain-damaged snow, heavy thaw/freeze cycles, icy surface
- Note:
  - 0 cm snowfall can still be Medium or High if conditions are stable.

Ice risk:
- High:
  - rain >= 0.3 inches + freeze OR strong ice signals
- Medium:
  - partial thaw/refreeze or moderate rain impact
- Low:
  - no rain and no freeze-thaw signals
- Exception:
  - if snowfall significantly exceeds prior rain, reduce ice risk.

Lift hold risk:
- High:
  - wind >= 30 mph OR wind_hold_risk_flag = true OR mountain report indicates holds
- Medium:
  - elevated wind but no confirmed holds
- Low:
  - normal wind conditions
- Note:
  - distinguish weather-related lift risk vs off-hours closure.

Temperature comfort:
- High:
  - comfortable skiing temperatures
- Medium:
  - cold but manageable
- Low:
  - extreme cold or dangerous wind chill

Rain impact:
- High:
  - significant rain during or before ski day
- Medium:
  - light rain with possible surface degradation
- Low:
  - no rain
- Note:
  - rain impact is primarily through snow damage and icing.

Mountain report usage:
- Treat mountain report text as a strong operational signal.
- Use it to interpret:
  - lift status anomalies
  - grooming conditions
  - snow quality descriptions
  - weather impacts
- Prefer mountain report context over isolated conflicting metrics.

Reddit usage:
- Only a confirmation signal.
- Do NOT override structured data.
- If Reddit disagrees → slightly reduce confidence.

Decision guidance:
- “Go” if:
  - resort is operational AND
  - no major combination of rain damage, extreme ice, dangerous wind, or shutdowns.

- “No Go” if:
  - conditions are unsafe OR
  - operations are significantly disrupted.

- If signals conflict:
  - choose the most operationally plausible interpretation
  - reduce confidence instead of forcing a hard conclusion.
